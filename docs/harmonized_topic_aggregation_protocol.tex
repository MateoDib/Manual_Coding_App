\section*{Purpose}

This document provides a printable summary of the coding grammar. It deliberately contains no participant responses, interview excerpts, row-level codings, or empirical database content.

The applications support more than topic coding. They encode qualitative material as ordered, DAG-compatible topic sequences that preserve mechanisms, definitions, branches, trade-offs, priorities, source markers, and actor-specific scopes.

\section*{Core Operators}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{L{0.18\linewidth} L{0.74\linewidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Operator} & \textbf{Interpretation} \\
\midrule
\inlinecode{-->} & Positive causal or consequential link. \\
\inlinecode{=} & Definition, equivalence, or framing. \\
\inlinecode{;} & Distinct narrative path in the same response. \\
\inlinecode{+} & Additive package or bundle. \\
\inlinecode{\&} & Coexistence without additive meaning. \\
\inlinecode{<} / \inlinecode{>} & Priority or dominance relation. \\
\inlinecode{|} & Information source marker, for example \inlinecode{A --> B | newspaper}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section*{Private Workbook Flow}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Manual coding uses \inlinecode{coding\_interview\_base.xlsx}.
\item After all coders have added their columns, copy it to \inlinecode{harmonization\_interview\_base.xlsx}.
\item Harmonization writes the final base to \inlinecode{harmonized\_interview\_base.xlsx}.
\end{enumerate}
